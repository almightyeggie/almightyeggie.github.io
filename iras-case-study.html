IRAS Tax Submission — Legacy to Modern

Redesigning and rebuilding a critical 3-step government tax filing flow from a 90s-era legacy system into JustLogin's modern SaaS platform — eliminating the need for users to switch between two separate systems.

Background

A critical workflow stuck in the past

IRAS (Inland Revenue Authority of Singapore) tax filing is a mandatory annual process for HR teams using JustLogin. Every year, HR administrators need to generate, review, and submit income tax forms — IR8A, Appendix 8A, and A8B — on behalf of their employees directly to the Singapore government.

When JustLogin launched its modern Express platform, most features were migrated over — but the IRAS submission module was left behind in JustLogin Classic, a legacy system built in the 90s with an outdated UI. This meant HR users had to maintain logins to both systems, context-switching mid-workflow just to complete their tax filing.

"Users had to log in to the old Classic system just for IRAS. The whole point of Express was to have everything in one place — this was a gap we needed to close."

The task was to design and build the full IRAS submission flow natively inside JustLogin Express — matching the modern design system, and making the 3-step process intuitive enough that HR admins could complete it without training.

The Flow

A 3-step submission process

The IRAS submission flow was structured as a clear, linear 3-step wizard — guiding HR admins from form generation through to final submission status. Each step had a distinct purpose and its own validation logic.

1
Generate IRAS Forms
Configure submission details & select employees
2
Review & Submit
Review history, validate, submit to IRAS
3
Submission Status
View results, warnings & error handling

Each step was connected by a persistent progress indicator at the top of the screen — a design decision that kept users oriented throughout the multi-step flow and reduced drop-off and confusion.

Process

Mockup to HTML to production

This project went through a full design-to-development lifecycle — from initial wireframe and mockup, through to coded HTML templates that were handed off directly to the engineering team for backend integration.

1

Mapped the existing Classic flow

Audited the legacy IRAS module in JustLogin Classic to understand the full scope — what fields were required, what validations existed, and what the 3-step sequence needed to cover including edge cases like warnings and failed submissions.

2

Designed within the Express system

Created mockups that strictly followed JustLogin Express's established design system — Bootstrap grid, typography, color palette, form components, and button styles. The goal was for IRAS to feel like it had always been part of Express, not a transplant.

3

Designed the 3-column form layout

Step 1 required a dense form with three distinct sections: IRAS Submission Details, Other Details, and Authorized Person Details. Structured these into a clean 3-column layout with clear grouping so HR admins could scan and fill efficiently without feeling overwhelmed.

4

Built the IRAS History table (Step 2)

Designed a filterable data table showing batch submissions by form type, tax year, status, and result counts — with download and delete actions per row. Included filter controls for Year and Form Type to handle large historical datasets.

5

Designed the error & warning states (Step 3)

Step 3 required careful handling of submission outcomes — success, failure, and warnings. Designed a clear status table with visual indicators, plus a dedicated warning section that surfaced specific field-level issues with contextual descriptions and gave users two clear paths: resubmit or proceed.

6

Built and handed off to dev

Translated all mockups into full HTML/CSS templates using the JustLogin Bootstrap framework, then collaborated with the engineering team during integration — troubleshooting any rendering or layout issues in the live environment.

Output

All 3 steps, shipped to production

Each screen was designed and built to handle real user scenarios — including validation errors, multi-row data tables, and complex form logic — all within JustLogin's Express design system.

Step 1 — Generate IRAS Forms
3-column form with submission config, employee selection table, and pagination. Users select employees and configure form type before generating.
Step 2 — Review & Submit
Filterable history table showing batch status, success/fail counts, and per-row actions. Users verify generated forms before submitting to IRAS.
Step 3 — Submission Status
Results dashboard with status indicators, warning descriptions, and two clear action paths — resubmit with corrections or proceed despite warnings.
